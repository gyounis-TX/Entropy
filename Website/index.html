About Lumen Innovations

Built by a physician who understands the challenges of modern medical practice.

Photo

George A. Younis, MD, FACC

Founder & Developer

Dr. George Younis is a board-certified interventional cardiologist with over 20 years of experience in clinical interventional cardiology and medical education. He currently serves as Program Director for the Interventional Cardiology Fellowship at Texas Heart Institute, where he previously served as Associate Program Director for the General Cardiology Fellowship. He has also held multiple leadership positions in cardiovascular quality improvement at Baylor St. Luke's Medical Center. Beyond these academic and institutional roles, Dr. Younis manages a private cardiology practice, giving him firsthand insight into the importance of accurate charge capture and revenue cycle optimization.

In helping to run both general and interventional cardiology fellowship programs, he became acutely aware of the limitations of existing software solutions for managing trainee procedure logs, analytics, and work hours documentation. He also recognized training programs' and ACGME's need for procedure attestation, meaningful trainee evaluation, and shared image repositories for teaching purposes—alongside attendings' universal desire to make everything they're required to do as quick and easy as possible so they can get back to the cath lab.

From running a private cardiology practice for over 20 years, Dr. Younis also became intimately familiar with the bottlenecks and pain points experienced by his clinical staff. This firsthand understanding of daily workflow challenges motivated him to develop AI-powered applications that make clinical processes simpler and more effective—not just for physicians, but for the entire care team.

His unique combination of clinical expertise, leadership in medical education and quality improvement, paired with a passion for solving problems with technology, positioned him to create apps that fill these unmet needs for physicians and training programs alike.

"Technology should make a physician's life easier. Clicks matter. Every feature in these apps exists to address a workflow gap I have experienced myself."
